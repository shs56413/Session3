Digital Safety

Verify

It is important to exercise caution and verify the credibility and trustworthiness of websites before interacting with them. Stick to well-known and reputable sources, look for secure connections (https://), read user reviews or ratings, and be mindful of any suspicious or unprofessional elements on the site.

Privacy

Sharing sensitive details like your full name, address, phone number, or financial information can put you at risk of identity theft, scams, or unauthorized access to your accounts.

Virus Protection

Installing virus protection also contributes to overall system performance. By removing malware and unwanted programs, it helps optimize your computer's speed and responsiveness.

Freebies

Unwanted Advertisements and Spam: Freebies often come with a catch. You may be required to provide your email address or other contact details, which can result in an influx of unwanted advertisements, spam emails, or even your information being sold to third-party marketers.

Password

To enhance password protection, students should follow best practices such as creating complex and unique passwords for each account, using two-factor authentication whenever possible, and regularly updating their passwords.

Information

Sharing personal information online can pose significant risks for students. It can make them vulnerable to cyberbullying, online harassment, or even identity theft
